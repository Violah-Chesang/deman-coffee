About Deman Coffee

DEMAN

Regenerative Agriculture

Deman Estate

Deman Estate is Situated on the eastern slopes of Mount Kenya, Nkubu town, Meru county-Kenya. It is one of the most fertile and best-watered parts of Kenya. It takes 3-6 hours to drive from Nairobi to Deman Estate.

Deman Estate is, in fact, not just one single farm, but spans two farms near each other. The farms mainly cultivate two coffee varieties: Ruiru 11 and Batian.

Meet the owner

Mr. Mutai Kinyua

Mr. Mutai Kinyua is an energetic and charismatic coffee producer. He lives in Nairobi with his family but spends a lot of his time on the farm in Meru, as well. Since the drive takes between 3 and 6 hours, depending on traffic, he usually stays for a couple of days at a time.

Mutai has been farming with regenerative agriculture principles (agroforestry) for 20 years since taking on the farm, and first undertook a transition from traditional agriculture processes.

Caltivation and Proccessing

Deman Farm

The variety of coffee at Deman Coffee Estate are cultivars Ruiru 11 and Batian.

We farm with regenerative agriculture agroforestry and the soil at our farm is a mixture of sand and soil rich in nutrients. We use as few chemicals as possible in our farming. The fertilizer we use is organic and consists, to a large extent, of the coffee pulp and parchment, which is combined with an organic fertilizer. We refrain from using pesticides and fungicides and instead use natural techniques.

Coffee Trees

We plant new coffee trees with seeds we buy from the Coffee Research Institute in Ruiru. Trees on the Deman Estate have a crop cycle every 7-12 years depending on the variety. We avoid uprooting trees at the 7-12 year mark. This has helped us maintain soil health over time. Instead of taking a lower-yielding tree out of the soil and planting a newer, younger tree in its spot, we select trees to cut down to a stump which stimulates the tree regrowing, renewing it entirely into a stronger and hardier unit. Processing & Drying

After the harvest, the coffee is sorted by grade. The beans are then divided into eight categories depending on their size. We process our coffee naturally which improves the taste and uses less water compared to the washed method. We ferment some of our coffees using the carbonic maceration process. (After harvesting, the cherries are placed in a tank and, without adding any water, they undergo an anaerobic fermentation for 2-3 days.) Next, they are laid out on drying beds (raised tables with meshes for table tops) and dried for a few weeks. We also use the honey process.
